# Human-Disease-Network-Analysis-with-Graph-Centrality-and-Community-Detection

This project analyzes a publicly available human disease-gene network to explore relationships between genetic factors and human diseases.\
A graph was consructed from the dataset, representing genes and diseases as nodes and their gentic associations as edges. Structural analysis was performed to study several aspects of the network, including topology, density, connectivity, and degree distribution.\
Several centrality measures (degree, betweenness, eignvector and closeness) were applied to identify the most influential nodes and understand their potential biological relevance.\
Community detection algorithms (Edge Betweenness, Leading Eigenvector, Louvain) were also applied to identify clusters of releated diseases, providing insights into genetic comorbidity patterns and potential shared biological mechanisms.
